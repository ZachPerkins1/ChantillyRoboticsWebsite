Subteams

Electrical

Electrical

Before build season, the electrical team tests sensors and prototypes various electrical boards. While the mechanical team is constructing the metal framework of the robot, the electrical team designs an electrical board that properly fits the CAD subteams's design. As the robot is being assembled, the electrical team connects all of its electrical components and installs its pneumatics system. At competitions, the electrical team works in the pit, primarily fixing loose wires and replacing batteries.

Mechanical

Mechanical

After prototyping potential designs, the mechanical team manufactures all the metal components of the robot. They assemble the robot and build game pieces so the robot can be tested during the last week of build season. During competition, the mechanical team serves as part of the pit crew for the robot, ensuring its metal parts stay structurally sound.

CAD

CAD

The CAD (Computer Aided Design) team serves two primary functions: design and fabrication. Using Solidworks, a computer software, the CAD team designs a three-dimensional model of the team’s robot to ensure it doesn’t exceed the game’s limitations. Working alongside the mechanical team and electrical team, they use this three-dimensional model to create the robot’s final blueprints. Additionally, C.A.D. fabricates various parts for the robot using a 3D printer, CNC, and laser cutter.

Programming

Programming

The programming team is divided into four divisions: vision, robot code, telemetry, and website. Each of these divisions work separately to complete their set goal. Vision codes a script on the roboRIO’s coprocessor so camera input can be processed during competition. The camera input allows the robot to lock onto set targets during both the autonomous and teleoperation stages of the competition. The robot code division writes, tests, and then rewrites all of the code that controls the robot. They work alongside our team drivers to ensure the robot is easy to operate and performs all of its necessary tasks. Telemetry creates a scouting application in order to attract other teams to join our alliance, and collects and graphs data of the robot during competition to assess its overall wellbeing. This constant input of data allows quick strategy decisions and improvements to be made during competition. The website division is in charge of updating and maintaining the team website. They work closely with the marketing subteams to implement the website content.

Marketing

Marketing

The marketing team handles team-wide community outreach, documentation, and overall image. The marketing team does a wide variety of tasks, ranging from advertising Chantilly Robotics Team 612 to the entire school to ensure a steady stream of future members, to contacting sponsors, to writing the Woodie Flowers Essay and completing the Chairman's Essay, Video, and Presentation.
