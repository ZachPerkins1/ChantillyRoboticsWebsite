Electrical picture

Electrical Subteam

Before build season, the electrical team tests sensors and prototypes various electrical boards. While the mechanical team is constructing the metal framework of the robot, the electrical team designs an electrical board that properly fits the C.A.D. subteams's design. As the robot is being assembled, the electrical team connects all of its electrical components and installs its pneumatics system. At competitions, the electrical team works in the pit, primarily fixing loose wires and replacing batteries.

Mechanical picture

Mechanical Subteam

After prototyping potential designs, the mechanical team manufactures all the metal components of the robot. They assemble the robot and build game pieces so the robot can be tested during the last week of build season. During competition, the mechanical team serves as part of the pit crew for the robot, ensuring its metal parts stay structurally sound.

CAD picture

CAD Subteam

The C.A.D. (Computer Aided Design) team serves two primary functions: design and fabrication. Using Solidworks, a computer software, the C.A.D. team designs a three-dimensional model of the team’s robot to ensure it doesn’t exceed the game’s limitations. Working alongside the mechanical team and electrical team, they use this three-dimensional model to create the robot’s final blueprints. Additionally, C.A.D. fabricates various parts for the robot using a 3D printer, CNC, and laser cutter.
